      ## Weekend hires, deposits, and site details

      Each marquee is a [daily listing](/features/daily-events/) with its
      own capacity per date, so the same marquee cannot be booked twice for
      the same weekend. With [multi-day hire](/features/multi-day-hire/),
      the customer chooses how long to keep it, and each day of the hire
      can have its own price.

      Marquee hires are large bookings, so
      [deposits](/features/deposits-and-balance-payments/) matter. Take
      part of the price at booking and send the customer a link to pay the
      balance before the event.

      [Custom questions](/features/custom-questions/) collect site details
      at checkout, such as ground type, access width, and whether the
      ground can be pegged. Customers agree to your
      [terms and conditions](/features/terms-and-conditions/) before they
      can book, and [postcode lookup](/features/postcode-lookup/) fills in
      the site address so your crew is not sent to a mistyped street.

      ## Set-up, teardown, and your crews

      The built-in [logistics system](/features/logistics/) gives every
      booking a drop-off assignment and a collection assignment, with
      optional times for each. An agent can be a crew, a van, or a single
      rigger.

      Crew members get their own agent-class login. They see only their
      run sheet, with the site address, a map link, and the customer's
      phone number, and they mark each job done as it happens.

      When a marquee comes back needing cleaning, repairs, or an
      inspection, a [servicing event](/features/servicing-events/) takes
      it out of availability without creating a customer booking, and the
      cost can be recorded in the [ledger](/features/ledger/).
      [Packages](/features/packages/) bundle a marquee with extras such as
      flooring, lighting, chairs, or a generator into one booking with one
      price.
