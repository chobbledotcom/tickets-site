      ## No booking fee on top of the ticket

      Many ticketing platforms add a booking fee of £1-2 to each ticket,
      so a £10 ticket costs the audience £11 or more. Chobble Tickets adds
      [no booking fee](/features/no-per-ticket-fees/), so the audience
      pays the ticket price you set and nothing else.

      The platform costs a flat £50/year or £5/month, with a 50% discount
      for charities and community groups, which covers most amateur
      dramatics societies. Payments go through Stripe, Square, or SumUp
      directly to the society's own account.

      A run of performances is set up either as a
      [daily event](/features/daily-events/) with per-date capacity, or by
      [duplicating an event](/features/duplicate-events/) for each
      performance.

      ## General admission only

      Chobble Tickets does not have seat maps or allocated seating. All
      tickets are general admission, so it suits venues where the audience
      picks their own seats. If your venue needs numbered seats, a
      platform with reserved seating is a better fit.

      Concession pricing works through separate listings in a
      [group](/features/groups/) with shared capacity, such as adult,
      child, and concession tickets counting against one house limit, or
      through [promo codes](/features/promo-codes-and-add-ons/) for
      members.

      Not everyone books online. [Manual attendees](/features/manual-attendees/)
      record door sales, and they appear in the same attendee lists and
      [CSV exports](/features/csv-export/) as online bookings.
