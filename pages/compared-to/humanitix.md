      # Chobble Tickets vs Humanitix

      Humanitix is a registered charity that donates its booking fee profits to education projects. This page compares it to Chobble Tickets on pricing, features and data handling.

      ## Pricing comparison

      [Humanitix](https://humanitix.com/gb/pricing) charges a percentage
      plus a fixed fee per paid ticket. There is no annual subscription. All
      plans include all features, and the platform donates 100% of its
      profits to charity.

      The Standard rate is 4.67% + £0.46 per paid ticket (excl. VAT),
      which includes payment processing through Humanitix Payments or
      Stripe. Registered charities and schools pay a reduced rate of
      3.33% + £0.25 per paid ticket. Free events cost nothing.

      Chobble Tickets is a flat £50/year or £5/month, with
      [no per-ticket platform fee](/features/no-per-ticket-fees/).
      Community groups, charities, and schools pay £25/year. Payment
      processing goes through the organiser's own Stripe, Square, or
      SumUp account at the provider's normal rate.

      Because Humanitix's fee is a percentage of the ticket price, it
      scales with both volume and price. On a £15 ticket at the Standard
      rate, the platform fee is around £1.16 per ticket. Sell 500 of
      those and the platform fee is around £580 for the year. Chobble
      Tickets costs £50 for the same volume.

      ## Charity structure

      Both Chobble and Humanitix direct profits away from shareholders.

      Humanitix is a registered charity. Its website states that 100% of
      profits go to education, healthcare and similar projects, and it
      had donated over £10.8 million as of July 2026. The donation is
      funded by the booking fees organisers pay.

      Chobble Tickets is a [Community Interest
      Company](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-introduction),
      a UK legal structure that locks the company's assets for community
      benefit. Profit cannot be extracted by shareholders. The difference
      is that Chobble's income comes from a flat annual hosting fee, not
      from a percentage of each ticket sold.

      ## Feature comparison

      ## Data ownership

      Humanitix stores attendee data on its servers. Like most hosted
      platforms, attendee details are readable by the platform. Humanitix
      also runs a discovery marketplace, so your events appear alongside
      other organisers' events, and attendees may receive emails about
      other events on the platform.

      With Chobble Tickets, attendee data is encrypted at rest and stays
      under your control. The organiser holds the only private key that
        can decrypt it. Chobble Tickets does not run a discovery
      marketplace and does not market other events to your attendees.

      ## When Humanitix might be the better choice

      - You want a discovery platform to help attendees find your events
      - You need reserved seating with a visual seat picker
      - You want waitlists for sold-out events
      - You need scheduled advance payouts before the event
      - You want built-in affiliate code tracking for promoters
      - You sell merchandise and want Canva design tools inside the event
        builder
      - You want direct integrations with Mailchimp, Salesforce or
        Facebook without setting up webhooks
      - You want attendees to be able to resell tickets through Tixel
      - You have very low ticket volume and prefer paying per ticket over
        a flat fee
      - You want a phone-based support team

      ## When Chobble Tickets is the better choice

      - You already have your own audience and don't need a discovery
        platform
      - You want predictable, flat-rate pricing with no per-ticket
        platform fees, so costs stay the same as volume grows
      - Privacy and encryption matter to you, and you want attendee data
        that the platform itself cannot read
      - You're a community group, charity, or school (£25/year)
      - You want [Apple and Google Wallet](/features/apple-wallet/)
        passes, [calendar feeds](/features/rss-and-calendar-feeds/), or
        a [public API](/features/public-api/) included
      - You want [custom email providers](/features/email-providers/) so
        confirmation emails come from your own domain
      - You value [open source](/features/open-source/) and the ability
          to read the source code, fork it, or run your own copy

      ## Who owns Humanitix?

      Humanitix was founded by Adam McCurdie and Josh Ross in Australia.
      It is a registered charity, not a for-profit company. Its website
      states that 100% of profits go to education, healthcare and
      community projects, and that over £10.8 million had been donated
      as of July 2026. The platform operates in Australia, New Zealand,
      the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom and Fiji.

      Chobble Tickets is run by one person as a
      [Community Interest Company](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-introduction)
      (CIC), a UK legal structure that locks the company's assets for
      community benefit. All of Chobble's code is public under
      [AGPL-3.0-only](/features/open-source/) with no enterprise tier or
      feature gates, and the platform can be self-hosted by anyone
      without depending on Chobble as a company.
