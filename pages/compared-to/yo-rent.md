      # Chobble Tickets vs Yo!Rent

      Both platforms can handle rentals, but at different scales. Yo!Rent builds full rental marketplaces. Chobble Tickets handles hire bookings for a single operator.
  - type: markdown
    content: |
      ## Two approaches to rentals

      [Yo!Rent](https://www.yo-rent.com) is rental marketplace software. It
      is built by FATbit Technologies as a ready-made product that you buy
      once and run on your own server. People use it to launch a website
      where many owners list physical items for rent, such as cars, camping
      gear, cameras, party furniture, tools, and heavy equipment. It
      supports single-vendor stores, multi-vendor marketplaces, and
      peer-to-peer rental sites.

      Chobble Tickets is an event ticketing platform that also handles
      hire. Each listing has a type, and setting the type to hire switches
      the default options to hire ones. Combined with delivery and
      collection scheduling and the ability to block out items that are out
      of action, this lets a single operator take bookings for their own
      equipment. One of Chobble Tickets' customers,
      [Elliott's Bouncy Castle Hire](https://www.elliottsbouncycastlehire.co.uk/news/2026-02-13/new-ticket-platform-initial-review),
      runs a hire business on it.

      The difference is scale and model. Yo!Rent is for building a rental
      business where the public, or many separate vendors, browse a
      catalogue and rent items. Chobble Tickets is for one operator hiring
      out their own items on set dates, often alongside events.

      ## How Chobble Tickets handles hire

      A hire booking works like an event booking with a date and a limited
      number of units. When you set a listing's type to hire, the options
      default to hire settings rather than ticket ones. A customer picks a
      date, and the item is held for that booking so it cannot be
      double-booked.

      Three features make this work for a hire business:

      - **[Delivery and collection](/features/logistics/)** - each booking
        can have a drop-off and a collection, assigned to an agent (a van,
        a driver, or a crew). Agents get a run sheet on their phone showing
        the address, phone number, a map link, and a "mark done" toggle for
        each drop-off and collection.
      - **[Servicing holds](/features/servicing-events/)** - when an item
        goes out for repair or cleaning, you block its capacity for those
        dates without creating a customer booking, so nobody can hire it
        while it is out of action.
      - **[Accounting ledger](/features/ledger/)** - record the costs tied
        to each item, such as repairs, replacement parts, or fuel, so each
        listing's profit reflects both income and costs.

      This suits a business hiring out its own stock. It does not turn
      Chobble Tickets into a multi-vendor marketplace, and it does not add
      per-item deposits, late fees, or formal rental contracts. Yo!Rent
      does all of those.

      ## Pricing comparison

      Yo!Rent charges a one-time licence fee rather than a subscription or
      a per-transaction cut. You pay once, get the software, and host it
      yourself. There are no recurring platform fees and no per-rental
      commission. The listed prices below are the current promotional
      prices, which are lower than the full recommended prices shown on
      the Yo!Rent site.

      | Yo!Rent package | Price (one-time) | Approx. GBP | What it includes |
      |---|---|---|---|
      | GoQuick | $1,499 | ~£1,180 | Standard web platform, self-hosted, white-label, 12 months support |
      | GoCustom | $6,749 | ~£5,300 | GoQuick plus custom design (up to 9 pages) |
      | GoQuick Boost | $2,999 | ~£2,360 | GoQuick plus buyer apps for Android and iOS (app source code not included) |
      | GoQuick Ultra | $6,999 | ~£5,500 | GoQuick Boost plus the mobile app source code |

      Add-on services include standalone buyer mobile apps ($1,999),
      mobile app source code ($4,999), and hourly custom development
      through the GoCustom Prime tier. Payment processing is handled by
      your own payment gateway (Yo!Rent lists 20+ pre-integrated
      gateways), which charges its own fees on top.

      Chobble Tickets is a flat **£50/year (or £5/month)** for the whole
      site, with [no per-booking fees](/features/no-per-ticket-fees/).
      Community groups, charities, and schools pay £25/year on the annual
      plan. Stripe processing is charged separately at the standard 1.5% +
      20p. Self-hosting Chobble Tickets is free, because the code is open
      source under AGPLv3 with no licence fee.

      The two pricing models are hard to compare directly. Yo!Rent is a
      large one-time payment for a rental platform you keep forever.
      Chobble Tickets is a small recurring fee, or free if you self-host.
      The right comparison is the scale of business each one suits.

      ## Feature comparison

      Both platforms handle rentals, but Yo!Rent is built for running a
      rental marketplace and Chobble Tickets for a single operator hiring
      out their own items. Here is the breakdown:

      **Both platforms share some features:**

      - Rentals booked for a date, with the item held so it cannot be
        double-booked
      - Online payment collection through a payment gateway
      - A self-hosting option, so data can stay on your own server
      - White-label branding, so the platform carries your name
      - An admin dashboard for managing bookings
      - Email confirmations to customers
      - No per-transaction platform commission

      **Yo!Rent has features Chobble Tickets does not:**

      - **Multi-vendor marketplaces** - let many sellers list their own
        items and take a commission on each rental
      - **Peer-to-peer rentals** - run a site where individuals rent items
        to each other
      - **Large product catalogue** - listings with images, categories, and
        search across many rentable items
      - **Per-item availability calendars** - track availability item by
        item across a big inventory
      - **Security deposits** - hold a refundable deposit against damage or
        late return
      - **Late fees and return handling** - charge for overdue returns and
        record the condition of returned items
      - **Rental agreements** - generate and store rental contracts for
        each booking
      - **Buyer and seller messaging** - built-in chat between renters and
        owners
      - **Native mobile apps** - Android and iOS apps for buyers, with app
        source code available on higher tiers
      - **Request for quote** - let customers ask for a custom price on
        high-value or bulk rentals
      - **Shipping and distance calculations** - work out delivery costs
        based on distance
      - **Reviews and ratings** - customers rate items and sellers
      - **Rent-and-sell hybrid** - list some items for sale and others for
        rent on the same site

      **Chobble Tickets has features Yo!Rent does not:**

      - **[Delivery and collection run sheets](/features/logistics/)** -
        assign each drop-off and collection to an agent, who sees a phone
        run sheet with the address, map link, and a "mark done" toggle
      - **[Servicing holds](/features/servicing-events/)** - block an
        item's dates while it is out for repair or cleaning, without
        creating a customer booking
      - **[Double-entry accounting ledger](/features/ledger/)** - record
        income and costs against each item so its profit is tracked
      - **[Event ticketing in the same tool](/features/selling-tickets/)** -
        run ticketed events alongside hire, with QR check-in
      - **[QR code check-in](/features/qr-code-check-ins/)** - scannable
        codes for events and bookings
      - **[Apple and Google Wallet](/features/apple-wallet/)** - customers
        can add bookings to the Wallet app on their phone
      - **[Pay-what-you-want pricing](/features/stripe-and-square/)** - let
        customers pick their own price above a minimum
      - **[Promo codes, discounts, and add-ons](/features/promo-codes-and-add-ons/)** -
        create promo codes, surcharges, and opt-in add-ons with stock limits
      - **[End-to-end encryption](/features/encrypted/)** - customer data

      - **[Custom email providers](/features/email-providers/)** - send

      - **[RSS and calendar feeds](/features/rss-and-calendar-feeds/)** -
        publish a feed of your listings that customers can follow
      - **[Refund processing](/features/refunds/)** - issue refunds from

      ## Data ownership and licensing

      Both products can keep your data on your own server, but the
      licences are different.

      Yo!Rent is proprietary software sold with a one-time licence. You
      buy the product, get the source code, and install it on your own
      hosting, so customer data lives on infrastructure you control. The
      source code comes with a commercial licence, which is not the same
      as open source. You can run and modify your copy, but you cannot
      freely redistribute the code, and the higher tiers charge extra for
      the mobile app source code.

      Chobble Tickets is open source under AGPLv3. Every line of code that
      runs at tix.chobble.com is in the public
      [GitHub repository](https://github.com/chobbledotcom/tickets). You
      can read it, fork it, and run the same software on your own server
      for free. Customer data is also encrypted at rest, so even someone
      with access to the database cannot read names or emails without your
      key.

      Both approaches give strong data ownership when self-hosted. The
      difference is that Yo!Rent charges a large one-time fee for a
      proprietary licence, while Chobble Tickets is free to self-host
      under an open source licence.

      ## When Yo!Rent is the better choice

      - You are building a rental marketplace where the public browses a
        catalogue and rents items
      - You want a multi-vendor or peer-to-peer site where others list
        their own items and you take a commission
      - You have a large inventory of many different products with per-item
        availability, images, and search
      - You need security deposits, late fees, and formal rental agreements
      - You want native mobile apps for your customers
      - You are happy to pay a large one-time fee and host the software
        yourself

      ## When Chobble Tickets is the better choice

      - You are one business hiring out your own items, such as bouncy
        castles, marquees, or AV gear, and taking bookings by date
      - You run events or ticketed activities alongside hire and want one
        tool for both
      - You need delivery and collection scheduling with a driver run sheet
      - You want [QR check-in](/features/qr-code-check-ins/) and
        [Apple or Google Wallet](/features/apple-wallet/) passes
      - You want a small flat fee, or free self-hosting, rather than a
        large one-time payment
      - You value [open source](/features/open-source/) code and
        [encryption](/features/encrypted/)
      - You are a community group, charity, or school (£25/year)

      ## Who makes Yo!Rent?

      Yo!Rent is developed by FATbit Technologies, a software company that
      sells ready-made products for launching online marketplaces. Yo!Rent
      is sold as a self-hosted product with a one-time licence fee, aimed
      at entrepreneurs starting rental businesses.

      Chobble Tickets is run by one person as a UK
      [Community Interest Company](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-introduction)
      (CIC), a legal structure that locks the company's assets for
      community benefit. All of Chobble's code is public under AGPLv3, and
      the platform can be self-hosted by anyone without depending on
      Chobble as a company.
